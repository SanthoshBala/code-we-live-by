# Bug Report: 18 U.S.C. § 1001 — 2026.05.15

**CWLB release point**: 113-21 (revision_id=1, effective_date=2013-01-01)  
**OLRC reference**: `xml_usc18@119-90.zip` (text of § 1001 unchanged since 2006-07-27)

## Bug 1 (Critical): Missing continuation/penalty clause in text_content

### Description

The CWLB `text_content` and `provisions` for 18 U.S.C. § 1001(a) are missing the
concluding penalty clause. The USLM XML represents this as a `<continuation>` element
immediately following enumerated items (1)–(3) inside subsection (a). The parser does
not emit this element, so the entire penalty provision is silently dropped.

### CWLB text_content (truncated — ends at item (3))

```
(a) Except as otherwise provided in this section, whoever, in any matter within the
jurisdiction of the executive, legislative, or judicial branch of the Government of the
United States, knowingly and willfully—
    (1) falsifies, conceals, or covers up by any trick, scheme, or device a material fact;
    (2) makes any materially false, fictitious, or fraudulent statement or representation; or
    (3) makes or uses any false writing or document knowing the same to contain any
        materially false, fictitious, or fraudulent statement or entry;
(b) Subsection (a) does not apply to a party to a judicial proceeding ...
```

### OLRC authoritative text (USLM `<continuation>` element, omitted from CWLB)

```
shall be fined under this title, imprisoned not more than 5 years or, if the offense
involves international or domestic terrorism (as defined in section 2331), imprisoned
not more than 8 years, or both. If the matter relates to an offense under chapter 109A,
109B, 110, or 117, or section 1591, then the term of imprisonment imposed under this
section shall be not more than 8 years.
```

### Impact

The penalty provision of § 1001(a) is entirely absent. A reader sees the elements of
the offense but no punishment, making the section appear legally incomplete.

The same gap likely affects every section that uses the `<continuation>` element
pattern — common throughout Title 18 enumerated offenses.

### Suggested test

```python
def test_section_18_1001_includes_penalty_continuation():
    section = get_section(18, "1001")
    assert "shall be fined under this title" in section["text_content"]
    assert "imprisoned not more than 5 years" in section["text_content"]
    assert "chapter 109A" in section["text_content"]
```

## Bug 2 (Moderate): Notes parser collapses 17 distinct notes into one

### Description

The OLRC USLM XML for § 1001 contains 17 separate `<note>` elements, each with its
own `<heading>`. The CWLB `notes` array returns all of them concatenated under a single
entry with header `"Historical and Revision Notes"`, losing all other headings.

### OLRC note headings (17 distinct notes)

1. Historical and Revision Notes
2. *(no heading — editorial separator)*
3. Amendments
4. *(no heading — editorial separator)*
5. Change of Name
6. Short Title of 2004 Amendment
7. Short Title of 2003 Amendment
8. Short Title of 2000 Amendment
9. Short Title of 1998 Amendments
10. Short Title of 1996 Amendment
11. Short Title of 1994 Amendment
12. Short Title of 1990 Amendment
13. Short Title of 1989 Amendment
14. Short Title of 1988 Amendment
15. Short Title of 1986 Amendment
16. Short Title of 1984 Amendment
17. Short Title of 1982 Amendment

### CWLB notes array

```json
"notes": [
  {
    "header": "Historical and Revision Notes",
    "content": "Based on title 18, U.S.C., 1940 ed. ... Amendments\n\n2006—... Change of Name\n\n... Short Title of 2004 Amendment\n\n..."
  }
]
```

All 17 notes are concatenated into one entry. The `Amendments` heading, `Change of
Name` heading, and all 13 `Short Title of XXXX Amendment` headings are embedded in
the content string rather than surfaced as separate array entries.

### Impact

- Amendment history (most commonly queried note) is not addressable by key.
- Popular-name lookups (e.g. "Computer Fraud and Abuse Act of 1986") require
  text-searching the entire notes blob rather than filtering by heading.
- Affects all sections with multiple notes — likely widespread.

### Suggested test

```python
def test_section_18_1001_notes_are_distinct():
    section = get_section(18, "1001")
    note_headers = [n["header"] for n in section["notes"]["notes"]]
    assert "Amendments" in note_headers
    assert "Change of Name" in note_headers
    assert any("Short Title" in h for h in note_headers)
    assert sum(1 for h in note_headers if h == "Historical and Revision Notes") == 1
```
